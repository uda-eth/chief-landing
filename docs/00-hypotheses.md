# 00. Hypotheses

Three hypotheses for the Chief 5-day sprint. Copy these into the Setup tab of the candidate sheet.

---

## Primary Target User Hypothesis

I believe the first users are **iPhone-using solo founders and 2-to-3 person operator teams running services or productized-services businesses, ages 25 to 34, pre-seed to Series A**, because they experience **principal-time bottlenecks: every billable hour they lose to admin, scheduling, follow-ups, and pipeline hygiene is revenue they cannot recover.** They feel this pain daily and have already cobbled together Notion, ChatGPT, and a calendar app to half-solve it. I expect to find them in **founder Discord/Slack communities, Reddit founder subs, LinkedIn (filtered to "founder" + services or agency), and on Snapchat targeting iOS-only with Entrepreneur and Business interest stacks**.

### Why this ICP

1. The product is iMessage-native. iPhone is non-negotiable. That removes ~45 percent of the US market and roughly all of cost-sensitive global users, so the remaining segment must value time more than they value avoiding a new tool.
2. Services founders price by the hour or by the engagement. The cost of admin overhead is denominated in lost revenue, not just hours. That makes the ROI math obvious in a single sentence.
3. Tony's warm network skews to this exact profile (Sam at Fireflies, Emmanuel and Isaiah at Shield, UATX founders). Warm intros are highest-quality leads in a 5-day sprint and they cluster in this ICP.

---

## Primary Channel Hypothesis

I believe the highest-yield channels are, in priority order: **warm intros and referrals from Tony's existing network, founder-dense Discord and Slack communities (Indie Hackers Discord, Founder Cafe, Hustle Cafe, Lunadio), targeted Reddit posts in r/Entrepreneur and r/SaaS and r/SideProject, LinkedIn DMs to filtered founder lists, and paid Snapchat ads as a controlled experiment to measure cold-traffic conversion.**

### Why these channels

1. Warm intros convert at 10x cold for a beta launch. Tony already has UATX and Shield/Fireflies relationships, so this is the fastest path to qualified leads.
2. Founder communities trade in honest feedback and pre-launch testing. The Indie Hackers Discord and similar groups explicitly welcome beta requests when framed as feedback, not promotion.
3. Snapchat is the assigned paid channel. The interest is less about volume and more about learning whether a 25-34 iPhone-using audience can be reached at a workable CAC for a niche B2B-adjacent product. That data is itself the deliverable.
4. LinkedIn DMs scale better than X for founder outreach when the message is short and specific. X is used for ambient awareness, not direct response.

---

## Primary Message Hypothesis

I am testing whether the message **"your chief of staff, in iMessage. Stop losing billable hours to admin."** gets them to sign up.

The frame is performance, not wellness. The pain is dollars, not stress. The hook is the surface (iMessage) because no one else owns that surface.

### Why this message

1. The product surface is the wedge. "In iMessage" is the only sentence in the pitch that competitors cannot copy in a week. Lead with it.
2. Founders respond to revenue-denominated pain. "Billable hours" or "ops drag" beats "feel less overwhelmed" by a wide margin in services-business copy.
3. The proof point that lands fastest is the Obsidian vault. It signals data ownership, not lock-in, which matches the founder's instinct to never trust a closed system.

### Secondary messages to test against the primary

- **Identity frame:** "Founders run companies. They don't run their inboxes."
- **Time frame:** "What used to be a $120k chief of staff is now a text thread."
- **Trust frame:** "Every message gets saved as markdown to your iCloud vault. You own the brain."

If Day 2 signal shows the primary message underperforming the identity frame, swap the Snap creative B copy and the Reddit post angle on Day 3.
